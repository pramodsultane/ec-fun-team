My Himalayan Adventure in Manali

✍️ By Akshaykumar Sonune 📅 March 15, 2026 ⏱️ 5 min read
🏔️

The moment I stepped out of the cab in Manali, the cold breeze hit my face and I knew this was going to be special. Snow-capped peaks everywhere, the sound of the river Beas flowing by, and the smell of freshly brewed chai from a nearby stall - it was picture perfect.

Day 1: Arrival and Solang Valley

After checking into a cozy wooden cottage, I headed straight to Solang Valley. The 40-minute drive through pine forests was breathtaking. At Solang, I tried paragliding for the first time! Flying over the snow-covered valley with the mountains all around - an experience I'll never forget.

Day 2: Trek to Jogini Falls

Started early morning with a trek to Jogini Falls. The trail goes through apple orchards and small villages. Met a local family who offered me hot tea and told me stories about life in the mountains. The waterfall at the end was worth every step - crystal clear water falling from 50 feet!

Day 3: Old Manali and Cafes

Spent a day exploring Old Manali - narrow lanes, hippie shops, and the famous cafes. Had the most amazing pancakes at Dylan's Cafe while watching the sunset. Also visited Hadimba Temple, surrounded by tall deodar trees.

Tips for Fellow Travelers

  • Best time to visit: March-June for pleasant weather, December-February for snow
  • Pack warm clothes even in summer - evenings get chilly
  • Try local food: Siddu (steamed bread) and Thukpa (noodle soup)
  • Book stays in advance during peak season
  • Carry cash - many small shops don't take cards

Final Thoughts

Manali isn't just a destination, it's a feeling. The mountains teach you to slow down, breathe, and appreciate the little things. Can't wait for my next Himalayan adventure!
